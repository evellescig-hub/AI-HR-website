Introduction

In businesses today, companies are starting to use AI in every aspect. It is increasingly gaining more popularity in how they manage their employees and workplace systems. In Human Resources, AI is used for communications-based tasks that use Natural Language Processing, as well as data driven functions like payroll and compensation automation, and predictive analytics. These systems help organizations, efficiency, reduce human error, and make informed decisions about the workplace.

In this presentation we will talk about how AI is used in HR, with a primary focus on NLP (Natural Language Processing), and sub focuses on other categories. We will go over how these technologies work, why companies are using them, go over some real life examples, discuss the advantages and disadvantages of these systems, and finally talk about the future impact of AI on human resources.

What is AI?

AI refers to artificial intelligence, which is a technology that enables computers and machines to simulate human learning, comprehension, problem-solving, decision-making, creativity, and autonomy. Applications and devices equipped with AI can see and identify objects. They can understand and respond to human language. They can learn from new information and experience. They can make detailed recommendations to users and experts. They can act independently, replacing the need for human intelligence or intervention. A simple way to think about AI is a series of nested or derivative concepts that have emerged over more than 70 years.

How AI is used in HR?

Artificial intelligence in human resources means the application of AI technologies to transform traditional HR functions and processes. It involves using a combination of algorithms, machine learning models, and intelligent systems to automate repetitive tasks, gain deeper insights from HR data, and support decision-making across an organization. AI is the biggest change in HR in decades, reshaping the very foundation of how companies attract, manage, and retain talent. For years, HR teams have been burdened with manual processes, delayed decision-making, and limited visibility into workforce needs. Now, AI offers a way forward: faster operations, smarter insights, and more personalized employee experiences. By 2028, 92% of companies are expected to increase their investment in AI for HR, showing just how central this technology will be to the future of people’s operations. In the coming years, the use of AI will impact HR departments in two significant ways: First, by streamlining HR operations and leveraging workplace data to improve the talent planning and management process. Secondarily, AI will require HR departments to foster a change-minded culture capable of embracing new ways of working. There are many different types of AI’s that are used in HR like,

  • Chatbots and virtual assistants
  • Generative AI
  • Agentic AI (autonomous agents)
  • Predictive and prescriptive analytics
  • Natural language processing (NLP)

How it works

Natural Language Processing works by allowing computers to understand and analyze human language using specific software. Inside HR, NLP systems are responsible for large amounts of text data, like resumes, job descriptions, and employee feedback, so they can recognize patterns in language. The process involves breaking down text into smaller parts, so it can identify key works and meanings and then it interprets the overall context.

For example, when scanning a resume, NLP can detect relevant skills of experience and qualifications and match them to a specific job requirement. It uses specific phrases like “managed a team” to know that someone has leadership experience and then can weave out applicants that are not suitable for the job. It also analyzes employee surveys to determine whether feedback is positive or negative. Over time, these systems improve as they process more data, which makes them more accurate and efficient in the long run.

Why companies use it

Companies use Natural Language Processing in Human Resources because it allows them to handle large amounts of information quicky, accurately, and efficiently. HR departments handle hundreds of resumes, employee surveys, and communications, which would cost a lot of time and money to review manually. NLP can also reduce human error and will improve consistency in hiring and performance evaluation. In addition, NLP allows companies to understand employee feedback which can help them response to issues like dissatisfaction and stress more effectively. Overall, companies use NLP to save time, reduce costs, and gain a deeper understanding of their workplace.

Pros & Cons

AI in Human Resources has many benefits but also some problems. One advantage is that AI can save time by quickly checking resumes and handling large amounts of data. It also reduces work for HR staff and helps companies make better decisions using data. However, AI also has some disadvantages. It can be biased if the data is not fair. It may also cause privacy concerns because it uses personal information. In addition, AI cannot fully understand people’s emotions or personality. Because of this, companies should not rely only on AI and still need human judgment.

Pros

  • Faster hiring
  • Saves time
  • Less work for HR
  • Better decisions

Cons

  • Can be biased
  • Privacy problems
  • No human understanding
  • Too much reliance on AI

Conclusion

Artificial Intelligence is transforming Human Resources by making processes faster, more efficient, and more data driven. It helps organizations save time, reduce errors, and improve hiring and employee management. However, AI also comes with challenges such as bias, privacy concerns, and lack of human judgment. Because of this, the document emphasizes that AI should support—not replace—human decision-making.
